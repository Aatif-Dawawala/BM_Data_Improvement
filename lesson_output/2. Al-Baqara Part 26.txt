In this final part of Surah Al-Baqarah, we learn what it means to place our complete trust in Allah. Every concern, trial, and hope belongs to Him alone. True faith is not only belief but full surrender, where the heart finds peace in Allah’s wisdom and mercy.
These verses beautifully conclude Surah Al-Baqarah by summarizing faith, justice, mercy, and complete reliance on Allah. After warning against riba (interest), Allah promises eternal reward for those who believe, pray, and give charity sincerely, as explained in Tafsir Ibn Kathir. Furthermore, He calls believers to act justly and to trust Him in all matters.
The verse “Allah does not burden a soul beyond what it can bear” reassures us that every test is measured with divine wisdom, as noted in Tafsir al-Razi. Finally, the closing supplication — “Forgive us, pardon us, have mercy on us” — reflects the believer’s humility and total surrender to Allah’s forgiveness and protection.
