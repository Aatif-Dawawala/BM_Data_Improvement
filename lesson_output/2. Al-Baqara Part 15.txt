As we continue exploring this surah, we meet a powerful spiritual truth: food shapes the soul. 
What we consume does not only feed the body it affects the heart’s ability to remember Allah SWT. Each meal becomes an act of worship that can strengthen or weaken our spiritual state.
In these verses, Allah SWT reveals how fasting trains both the heart and the will. Shayṭān calls people toward temptation, carelessness, and harmful desires, yet fasting restores self-control and awareness of Allah. Ibn Kathīr explains that fasting is not simply leaving food and drink; it is a deliberate act of devotion that nurtures <i>taqwā</i> and brings the believer back to spiritual focus.
Furthermore, Al-Rāzī notes that fasting was prescribed to earlier nations as a mercy, giving people strength, gratitude, and balance. The moment of breaking the fast reminds believers that true freedom comes from obedience, moderation, and remembering Allah. Through this discipline, the body is restrained, the heart purified, and the soul drawn closer to its Lord.
