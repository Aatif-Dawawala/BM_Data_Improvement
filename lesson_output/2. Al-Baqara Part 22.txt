As we continue through Surah Al-Baqarah 249–254, we learn how divine tests reveal true faith, build patience, and teach reliance on Allah. Trials purify the heart and show that victory comes by Allah’s will.
Beautifully narrated, these verses tell Ṭālūt’s army facing a river: only a few restrained themselves and proved their faith (Ibn Kathir). When they faced Jālūt, victory came not from numbers but from Allah’s help for the steadfast (Al-Razi). Dāwūd’s victory shows Allah grants wisdom and rule to whom He wills. The passage ends by urging believers to give charity before the Day when wealth and friendship will not help a reminder of our duty to be sincere and generous (Ibn Juzay).
What was the test that Talut's army faced at the river? 
They had to cross a river without drinking from it - True 
They were ordered to fight Goliath at once - False 
They were commanded to build a defensive camp - False 
Explanation: The verses describe how the test was about drinking from the river; those who drank were not with Talut, showing their lack of faith. 
Why did some of Talut’s soldiers refrain from drinking the river water? 
To show their faith and obedience - True 
Because they were afraid of Goliath - False 
Because they were thirsty - False 
Explanation: Those who refrained showed trust in Allah and obedience, proving their sincerity. 
What did the soldiers pray for when they met Goliath? 
They asked Allah for patience and help - True 
They asked to run away - False 
They asked for forgiveness only - False 
Explanation: The verses record a prayer asking Allah for patience, steadfastness, and help against the disbelievers. 
Who defeated Goliath according to the verses? 
David (Dāwūd) - True 
Talut himself - False 
The soldiers ran away - False 
Explanation: Verse(s) say David killed Goliath with Allah’s permission. 
What did Allah grant David after his victory over Goliath? 
Sovereignty and wisdom - True 
A large army - False 
A special sword only - False 
Explanation: The passage states Allah gave David rule (sovereignty), wisdom, and what He willed (knowledge). 
Why does the Qur’an say some messengers were favoured above others? 
Because of their rank and signs from Allah - True 
Because they were stronger - False 
Because they fought each other - False 
Explanation: The verses explain Allah honoured some messengers with higher rank and signs; human conflict that followed was due to free will, not lack of signs.
