In these verses, Allah shows a painful truth, the danger of pretending to believe.
Some people said they had faith but didn’t mean it in their hearts. Allah gives clear examples to show that real belief needs honesty and light inside. Only sincere faith brings true peace.
Like a weak flame that shines for a moment then fades, these verses (13–24) talk about people who claimed to believe but secretly rejected faith. When told, “Believe as others have believed,” they laughed at the Prophet’s companions ﷺ and called them fools.
Ibn Kathir explains that “the people” means the Companions, whose simple and strong faith is the model for all believers.
 Al-Razi says the hypocrites were proud of their money and position, while real believers stayed humble and truthful.
Through the stories of fire and storm, Allah shows hearts that see the light but turn away from it. Faith without honesty can never last.
What did the hypocrites call the Prophet's Companions? 
Fools - True 
Enemies - False 
Weak people - False 
Explanation: “The hypocrites arrogantly called the Prophet’s Companions ﷺ ‘fools’ when asked to believe as they did. In truth, Allah declares that it is the hypocrites themselves who are foolish, though they do not realize it (Q. 2:13).” 
Who are 'the people' that others should believe like? 
The Prophet's Companions - True 
The tribal leaders - False 
The scholars of other religions - False 
Explanation: “According to Ibn Kathīr, ‘the people’ refers to the Companions of the Prophet ﷺ, whose faith and understanding of Islam form the eternal benchmark for true belief in every generation.” 
What made the hypocrites feel superior to the believers? 
Their wealth and social status - True 
Their knowledge of scripture - False 
Their age and experience - False 
Explanation: “Al-Rāzī explains that the hypocrites’ arrogance came from their wealth, leadership, and social influence, while most believers were humble and poor yet rich in faith.” 
Did the hypocrites truly believe in their hearts? 
Yes - False 
No - True 
Explanation: “The Qur’an reveals that the hypocrites only pretended to believe outwardly. Inwardly, they rejected the truth, thinking they could deceive Allah and the believers but only deceived themselves.” 
What leads to complete spiritual darkness according to the parables? 
Rejecting guidance after seeing it clearly - True 
Never hearing about guidance - False 
Being too young to understand - False 
Explanation: “According to the Qur’anic parables of fire and storm, those who recognize divine truth but still reject it are plunged into total spiritual darkness a state worse than ignorance itself.”
