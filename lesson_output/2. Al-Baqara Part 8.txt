Faith isn’t about convenience.
In these verses (2:85–96), Allah exposes a painful truth, some people obey only what suits them.
Real faith means surrendering even when the command challenges our comfort or pride, proving that belief is not about ease, but about trust in Allah’s wisdom.
In Madinah, the Jewish tribes disobeyed their own laws. They fought fellow Jews while allying with pagan tribes, then ransomed those captives, obeying one part of the law and breaking another (Ibn Kathir, Ma‘arif al-Qur’an).
Allah asked, “Do you believe in some parts of the Book and reject the rest?” Their selective obedience revealed that their faith was driven by self-interest, not devotion.
As Al-Razi explains, envy and pride deepened their blindness. They recognized Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as the promised messenger but rejected him because he wasn’t from their lineage.
Their story warns that clinging to pride and desire can turn belief into hypocrisy.
What contradiction did the Jewish tribes of Madinah show in their actions? 
They killed fellow Jews but paid ransom to free captives - True 
They prayed in public but sinned in private - False 
They claimed to love peace but started wars - False 
Explanation: Verse 85 describes how they fought and killed their fellow Jews, yet paid ransom to free them afterward. This contradiction exposed their selective obedience to divine law believing in part of the Scripture while rejecting the rest. 
Why did some people reject Prophet Muhammad ﷺ even though they recognized him from their scriptures? 
They didn't understand his message - False 
They were envious he wasn't from their lineage - True 
They preferred their old traditions - False 
Explanation: Verse 90 explains that envy (baghyan) led them to reject the Prophet ﷺ. They knew the truth but were resentful that Allah’s final messenger came from the descendants of Ismā‘īl rather than from their own lineage. 
What false god did the Israelites worship while Prophet Moses ﷺ was away? 
A golden eagle - False 
A calf - True 
A silver lamb - False 
Explanation: Verses 92-93 describe how they worshipped a golden calf a form of shirk while Moses was away. This act symbolized turning desire into a deity, following passions instead of divine guidance. 
What did the people say when asked to follow God's commands? 
We hear and we disobey - True 
We hear and we obey - False 
Explanation: Verse 93 records their arrogant words, “We hear and we disobey.” This phrase captures the spirit of rebellion outwardly listening but inwardly rejecting Allah’s commands. 
What is the fate of those who prefer worldly life over the Hereafter? 
They get a second chance - False 
Their torment will not be lightened - True 
They receive a warning first - False 
Explanation: Verse 86 states that those who trade the Hereafter for worldly gain will face a punishment that will never be reduced. This verse reminds us that worldly profit is worthless when it costs eternal peace. 
How did Allah strengthen Jesus, son of Mary (ʿĪsā ibn Maryam)? 
With angels from heaven - False 
With the Holy Spirit - True 
With powerful companions - False 
Explanation: Verse 87 mentions that Allah strengthened ʿĪsā ibn Maryam with the Rūḥ al-Qudus (Holy Spirit), referring to the angel Jibrīl (Gabriel), who brought divine guidance and protection throughout his mission.
