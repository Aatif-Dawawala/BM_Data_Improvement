As we continue our exploration of Al-Baqarah, we encounter a profound truth about worship.
How do we love Allah SWT with complete hearts while others divide their affection among false gods?
 This theme reveals the essence of pure faith - a love so strong it transforms every aspect of our spiritual lives.
In these verses (Al-Baqarah 2:157–168), Allah SWT teaches that righteousness is far deeper than rituals. It is not about turning toward a direction in prayer, but about a life rooted in sincere faith, compassion, and moral responsibility.
Moreover, true righteousness is believing in Allah, the Last Day, the Angels, the Book, and the Prophets, then living that belief through generosity, justice, and patience (Ibn Kathir). Al-Razi explains that acts of worship gain meaning only when guided by sincerity and service. Allah also warns against following the whispers of Shaytan, who deceives people into excess or neglect.
Consequently, real piety balances lawful enjoyment with gratitude, faith with good deeds, and devotion with care for others, the true spirit of Islam.
What do Safa and Marwa represent in these verses? 
Sacred rites of God for pilgrimage - True 
Elevated places historically associated with earlier communities - False 
Places where angels descended - False 
Explanation: Verse 158 clearly states that Safa and Marwa are among the rites of God for those making pilgrimage. 
Can those who hide God's guidance be forgiven? 
Yes - True 
No - False 
Explanation: Verse 160 shows God's mercy, He accepts repentance from those who repent, make amends, and declare the truth. 
What will happen between leaders and followers on judgment day? 
Leaders will disown their followers - True 
They will support each other - False 
Followers will protect their leaders - False 
Explanation: Verse 166 describes how those who were followed will disown their followers when they see the suffering. 
According to the verses, what are people instructed to consume from the earth? 
Food groups limited to vegetation and plant-based items - False 
What is wholesome, permitted, and free from corruption - True 
Only items approved by human religious authorities - False 
Explanation: Verse 168 instructs people to eat what is good and lawful from the earth, avoiding Satan's footsteps. 
According to the verses, why are people warned not to follow the footsteps of Shayṭān? 
Because he offers benefits that ultimately disappear - False 
Because he is a clear and open enemy to humanity - False 
Because his influence only brings short-lived distractions - True 
Explanation: Verse 168 warns against following Satan because he is described as humanity's sworn enemy.
