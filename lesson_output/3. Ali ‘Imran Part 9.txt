Imagine a chain of light stretching through time from Ādam to Muhammad ﷺ  each prophet passing the same truth forward.
 These verses remind us of a sacred promise: every prophet pledged to believe in and support the final Messenger. 
Islam is one message, one light, one covenant with Allah.
In a moment of divine history, Allah took a sacred <i>mithāq</i> - a covenant from every prophet, from Ādam to ʿĪsā (AS), that they would believe in and support the final Messenger ﷺ if he came in their time (3:81). <i>Ibn Kathīr</i> and <i>Ibn Juzayy</i> explain that this promise extended to every nation, binding all prophets and followers in one message of truth. 
<i>Al-Rāzī</i> adds that these verses exposed some among the People of the Book who hid this knowledge despite recognizing the Prophet’s signs.
Later, verses (3:86–89) mention al-Ḥārith ibn Suwayd, who left Islam but returned in repentance. His story shows that Allah’s mercy always welcomes sincere returners for every soul, by nature, is made to submit to Him, willingly or unwillingly (3:83).
