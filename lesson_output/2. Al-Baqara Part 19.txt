These verses teach how believers respond when Allah gives clear commands in matters of worship, conflict and family life. They remind us that faith is not only belief in the heart but obedience to guidance that protects our spiritual and worldly wellbeing.
In these ayahs, Allah responds to real situations faced by the early Muslim community. They asked the Prophet about fighting during sacred months, and Allah taught that although fighting then is serious, blocking people from His path and harming the believers is far worse. According to scholars like Ibn Kathir, these verses showed the believers that trials test sincerity. 
As we continue reading, the verses move into rules about marriage, waiting periods and reconciliation. This shift teaches that divine guidance covers public matters and private life. The Quran binds the community together through responsibility, mercy and justice, helping believers stay mindful of Allah in every decision.
Who are the people described in the verses as receiving Allah’s mercy? 
Individuals who prefer comfort and avoid the responsibilities of faith - False 
Individuals who sincerely believe, migrate for Allah, and strive in His cause - True 
Individuals who deny the truth and oppose the believers - False 
Explanation: The verses describe believers who show faith through belief, migration and striving in the path of Allah as receiving His mercy. 
What is the main message about intoxicants and gambling in these verses? 
They are completely forbidden with no benefits. - False 
They are allowed if people benefit from them. - False 
They contain great sin, and their sin is greater than their benefit. - True 
They are recommended for entertainment. - False 
Explanation: Verse 219 explains that both intoxicants and gambling are sinful, and the sin outweighs any benefit. 
What guidance do the verses give regarding menstruation and marital approach? 
Menstruation has no effect on marital interaction and can be ignored - False 
Menstruation is a natural difficulty and marital relations pause until purification - True 
Menstruation should be hidden and has no spiritual meaning - False 
Explanation: The verses explain that menstruation is a difficulty and marital relations pause until the woman becomes purified. 
What teaching do the verses give about the metaphor of spouses as fields? 
The metaphor teaches that marriage is only for companionship without responsibility - False 
The metaphor teaches lawful marital intimacy approached with respect and goodness - True 
The metaphor teaches that spouses have no choice in marital matters - False 
Explanation: The verses use a metaphor to teach that marital intimacy is lawful within respectful and good conduct. 
What does the verse say about oaths made in God's name? 
Oaths in God's name should always be kept at all costs - False 
Oaths in God's name should not hinder doing good and making peace - True 
Oaths in God's name can be broken whenever convenient - False 
Explanation: Verse 224 advises that oaths in God's name should not prevent us from doing good and making peace. 
What is the ruling for someone who swears not to approach their wife for four months? 
They must never return to their spouse under any condition - False 
They wait four months and may return, and Allah is forgiving if they do - True 
They should take another spouse without waiting any time - False 
Explanation: Verse 226 states that if someone swears not to approach their wife for four months, they should wait, and Allah is forgiving if they return.
