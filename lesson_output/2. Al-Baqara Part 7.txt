Some hearts don’t just forget, they harden.
In these verses (2:73–84), Allah reminds us that even after witnessing miracles, some people’s hearts became colder than rock. This passage teaches us how to keep our hearts alive through humility, reflection, and sincere faith before they turn lifeless through arrogance.
Allah revived a murdered man through the miracle of the sacrificed cow, proving His power to bring the dead to life (Ibn Kathir, Ma‘arif al-Qur’an). Yet, instead of believing, many hearts hardened.
Allah compared them to stones but said some stones are softer, for they split open to release water or fall from awe of Him (Al-Razi). Even creation shows more humility than those who deny truth.
The following verses reveal why hearts harden: twisting Allah’s words, hiding truth, and following faith selectively. Ibn Kathir explains that the cure lies in worship, kindness, and honesty, actions that polish the heart and restore spiritual life.
What miracle did Allah perform through the sacrificed cow? 
He brought a dead person back to life - True 
He made the cow speak to the people - False 
He turned the cow into gold - False 
Explanation: Verse 73 states that Allah brought a dead man back to life by striking him with a part of the sacrificed cow, showing His power to resurrect the dead and prove the reality of the Hereafter. 
How did people’s hearts react after witnessing this miracle? 
They became softer and more faithful - False 
They became as hard as rocks or harder - True 
They remained unchanged - False 
Explanation: Verse 74 explains that even after seeing the miracle, their hearts became as hard as rocks, or even harder. This shows that seeing signs doesn’t help if the heart refuses to humble itself before Allah. 
What did some people do after hearing Allah’s words clearly? 
They deliberately twisted them after understanding - True 
They forgot them completely - False 
They shared them with everyone - False 
Explanation: Verse 75 reveals that some people deliberately distorted Allah’s words after understanding them. This shows the danger of manipulating divine truth for worldly benefit or pride. 
Did Allah know what people were hiding in their hearts? 
Yes - True 
No - False 
Explanation: Verse 77 declares that Allah knows fully what people conceal in their hearts and what they reveal outwardly. Nothing is hidden from Him, He is Al-ʿAlīm, the All-Knowing. 
What was wrong with how some people understood the Scripture? 
They read it too slowly - False 
They knew it only through wishful thinking and guesswork - True 
They shared it with too many people - False 
Explanation: Verse 78 explains that some uneducated people relied only on wishful thinking and guesswork about their Scripture, rather than true knowledge. Faith without understanding easily turns into imagination. 
What punishment awaits those who falsely claim that their writings are from Allah? 
They will lose their wealth - False 
Woe to them for what they wrote and earned - True 
They will be given another chance - False 
Explanation: Verse 79 warns of destruction (wayl) for those who write false scripture with their own hands and claim, “This is from Allah,” just to gain wealth. This sin mixes deceit with arrogance toward revelation. 
Who will live forever in the Garden? 
Those who are wealthy and powerful - False 
Those who live the longest - False 
Those who believe and do good deeds - True 
Explanation: Verse 82 clearly states that those who truly believe and do righteous deeds will be the inhabitants of the Garden, living there forever in peace and reward.
