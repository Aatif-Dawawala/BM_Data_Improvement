As we move through this part of Al Baqarah, we learn how Allah teaches justice, sincere obedience, and His power to choose leaders through revelation. These verses guide our understanding of real strength, trust in Allah, and the meaning of true responsibility.
These verses teach two important lessons that link justice with leadership. Tafsir scholars explain that Allah first reminds believers to treat women fairly even after separation. This is described as a duty for those who wish to be righteous. The verses then guide us to the story of Bani Israil. 
They asked for a king, but their hearts were not fully sincere. Allah selected Talut because of his knowledge and strength, not wealth. Tafsir scholars mention that this choice tested their humility. Allah then returned the Ark to them as a sign of His support and a source of calm. 
These moments show that justice and sincere faith build a strong and righteous community.
Why did the Children of Israel object to Talut as their king? 
He was considered too young to guide their people - False 
He was not wealthy or from their royal families - True 
He did not wish to join them in their battles - False 
Explanation: The people objected because Talut lacked wealth and royal lineage, showing they judged by wrong standards. 
What does Allah value most when choosing leaders? 
Wealth and strong social connections in society - False 
Family background and respected ancestry - False 
Knowledge and inner capability in leadership - True 
Explanation: Allah chooses leaders based on knowledge and capability, not superficial qualities like wealth or family status. 
What did the Ark of the Covenant bring to the believers? 
Divine tranquility known as Sakinah - True 
Military weapons and physical equipment - False 
Explanation: The Ark brought divine tranquility (Sakinah) to the believers, providing spiritual strength when they accepted Allah's choices. 
What was the Children of Israel's main request to their prophet? 
To build them a new temple - False 
To appoint them a king for battle - True 
To lead them back to their homeland - False 
Explanation: After being driven from their homeland, the Children of Israel demanded a king to lead them in battle. 
What does this theme teach us about judging others? 
We should judge people based on outer appearance - False 
We should value character more than appearance - True 
Family background predicts a person’s ability - False 
Explanation: The lesson teaches us to value knowledge and character (substance) over superficial qualities like wealth or family status.
