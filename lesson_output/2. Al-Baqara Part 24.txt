This part of Surah Al-Baqarah teaches that faith cannot be forced and that sincere charity reflects true belief. Allah reminds us that both faith and generosity must come from the heart, seeking His pleasure alone. 
Our intentions and words determine whether our deeds are accepted or wasted.
These verses (2:256–264) explain that belief in Allah must be sincere and never compelled. As mentioned in <i>Tafsir Ibn Kathir</i>, the phrase “There is no compulsion in religion” means faith must arise from conviction and understanding. Allah is the Protector of believers, guiding them from darkness into light (<i>Tafsir al-Razi</i>).
The Qur’an then presents examples of faith in action through Prophet Ibrahim, showing trust in Allah’s power over life and death. Charity is compared to a seed that multiplies abundantly when given sincerely for Allah’s sake (<i>Ma‘arif al-Qur’an</i>). However, when giving is done for show or followed by hurtful words, its reward is lost. Sincere generosity grows only from pure intention and humility before Allah.
