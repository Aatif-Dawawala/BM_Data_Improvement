In this part of Surah Al-Baqarah, Allah teaches us how wealth can bring blessing or loss depending on our intention. True prosperity lies in giving sincerely for His sake and avoiding greed. Every act of spending becomes a reflection of faith and trust in Allah’s mercy.
In this section (2:265–276), Allah compares sincere charity to a fertile garden that flourishes under both heavy rain and gentle dew, always bearing fruit because it is rooted in sincerity and faith (<i>Tafsir Ibn Kathir</i>). In contrast, charity done to impress others is like dust on a rock, easily washed away (<i>Tafsir al-Razi</i>).
Furthermore, Allah warns against <i>riba</i> (interest), describing it as destructive and unjust. While charity brings growth and blessing, interest removes barakah and harms the soul (<i>Ma‘arif al-Qur’an</i>). Therefore, Allah reminds believers that every act of giving should be pure and selfless. Those who give for His sake will receive both worldly abundance and eternal peace in the Hereafter.

Explanation: The verses strongly contrast sincere giving with the dangers of Riba (interest) and greed, emphasizing their different outcomes.
