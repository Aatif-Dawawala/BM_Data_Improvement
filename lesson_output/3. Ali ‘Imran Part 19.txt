Have you ever seen people twist the truth to justify their wrongdoing?
These verses expose such arrogance.
Some falsely spoke about Allah, claiming He was poor or unjust. But Allah’s justice never fails. He permits falsehood for a time so that, in the end, truth alone stands clear and eternal.
In these verses, Allah ﷻ responds to the arrogance of some among the People of the Book who said, <i>“Allah is poor, and we are rich.”</i> (<i>Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr</i>) explains that this insult came after Allah encouraged charity, revealing their hypocrisy. Allah declares that He will hold them accountable for their words and their actions against His prophets.
<i>Al-Rāzī</i> writes that Allah allows arrogance to continue for a time, not out of weakness, but so that truth shines brighter when falsehood collapses.
According to <i>Maʿārif al-Qurʾān</i>, the real poor are those without guidance. Wealth and power are temporary tests, but Allah’s justice is perfect, and His judgment always comes at the right time.
