Not everything that looks divine comes from Allah.
In these verses (2:97–108), Allah teaches us how truth can be twisted by envy and deception. This lesson helps us see the difference between divine miracles and false magic, a guide to protect our hearts from confusion and lies.
When the Prophet ﷺ came to Madinah, some Jews rejected him because Angel Jibril brought the revelation, claiming they preferred Mikail (Ibn Kathir). Allah declared that to hate Jibril is to oppose Allah, for all angels act only by His command. They also accused Prophet Sulayman (Solomon) of magic, but Allah cleared him: “Sulayman did not disbelieve; the devils disbelieved, teaching people magic” (Al-Razi). The angels Harut and Marut were sent only as a test, warning, “We are a trial, so do not disbelieve” (Ma‘arif al-Qur’an).
Finally, Allah taught believers to speak with respect to say “Unzurna” instead of “Ra‘ina” showing that true faith includes good manners and pure speech.
Why did some Jews in Madinah reject Prophet Muhammad ﷺ even though they recognized his truth? 
They struggled with Arabic understanding and rejected the message - False 
They opposed the revelation because Jibrīl delivered it - True 
They preferred loyalty to their own prophet-lineage instead - False 
Explanation: According to the lesson and verse 97, some Jews rejected the Prophet ﷺ because Angel Jibrīl brought the revelation. They claimed to prefer Angel Mikā’īl, showing that their rejection came from prejudice, not ignorance. 
What does it mean to be an enemy to Angel Jibrīl (Gabriel)? 
It means committing a smaller wrong that is easily overlooked - False 
It means being an enemy to Allah, His angels, and His messengers - True 
It means that only Jibrīl himself would take personal offense - False 
Explanation: Verse 98 declares that enmity toward Jibrīl, Mikā’īl, or any of Allah’s messengers is enmity toward Allah Himself, because all angels act only by His command. 
What did Allah command believers to say instead of 'Ra'ina'? 
Unzurna - True 
Rahimna - False 
Explanation: Verse 104 commands believers to say Unẓurnā (“Please look at us”) instead of Ra‘inā, because the latter was twisted by enemies into a word of insult. This teaches believers to speak clearly and respectfully. 
Why did Allah forbid believers from using the word Ra‘inā when speaking to the Prophet ﷺ? 
It was considered overly casual speech in respectful settings - False 
Enemies used similar words to secretly insult the Prophet - True 
It carried an improper meaning in another Arabic usage - False 
Explanation: As explained in verse 104 and tafsir sources, some enemies used a Hebrew word that sounded like Ra‘inā to secretly insult the Prophet ﷺ. Allah replaced it with Unẓurnā to protect the Prophet’s honor and teach adab (proper manners). 
What does it reveal when people repeatedly break their covenants with Allah? 
Allah immediately punishes them - False 
It shows most of them do not truly believe - True 
Angels come to warn them personally - False 
Explanation: Verse 100 explains that whenever people break their covenants, it shows that most of them have no true faith in Allah’s guidance. Their repeated disobedience reflects a hardened heart, not mere forgetfulness.
