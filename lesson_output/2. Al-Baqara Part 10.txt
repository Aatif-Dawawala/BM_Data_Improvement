As we continue through Surah Al-Baqarah, we learn one of the hardest lessons, how to stay calm when others envy or criticize us.
These verses show how faith responds to jealousy and false claims not with anger, but with patience, wisdom, and trust in Allah’s plan.
Some among the People of the Book in Madinah recognized Prophet Muhammad ﷺ from their scriptures, yet rejected him out of envy (Ibn Kathir). They wished to turn believers away from Islam even after truth had become clear.
Their claims that only Jews or Christians would enter Paradise, that Allah had a son, or that worship must face one direction, were built on jealousy and pride, not revelation (Al-Razi).
Allah refuted them, declaring that salvation comes through sincere faith and good deeds, not lineage or titles (Ma‘arif al-Qur’an).
Jealousy blinds hearts, but faith brings peace and clarity to those who trust Allah’s mercy.
What motivated some People of the Book to wish believers would return to disbelief? 
Selfish envy, even after truth became clear - True 
Genuine concern for their salvation - False 
Fear of losing their religious authority - False 
Explanation: Verse 109 states that many among the People of the Book wished to turn believers back to disbelief “out of envy from themselves, even after the truth became clear.” This highlights that their motive was jealousy, not misunderstanding. 
According to these verses, who will truly enter Paradise? 
Only Jews, as they claimed - False 
Only Christians, as they claimed - False 
Anyone who submits wholly to God and does good - True 
Explanation: Verse 112 clarifies that Paradise is for “whoever submits himself wholly to Allah and does good.” Entry to Paradise depends on sincerity (ikhlāṣ) and righteous action (ʿamal ṣāliḥ), not group identity. 
How should believers respond when others criticize their faith out of envy? 
Argue back with strong evidence - False 
Forgive and show patience - True 
Avoid them completely - False 
Explanation: Verse 109 teaches believers to respond with patience and forgiveness: “Forgive and forbear until Allah brings His command.” This emphasizes moral restraint (ḥilm) and reliance on divine timing, not personal retaliation. 
What does ‘The East and the West belong to God’ teach us about worship? 
We can pray facing any direction we choose - False 
God is not limited to any specific direction - True 
Only certain places are suitable for prayer - False 
Explanation: Verse 115 reminds us that Allah’s essence is beyond space and direction: “Wherever you turn, there is the Face of Allah.” Worship is about obedience, not geography. 
What did both Jews and Christians falsely claim about each other? 
That the other group had no ground to stand on - True 
That the other group would enter Paradise - False 
Explanation: Verse 113 reveals that both groups declared the other to have “no true foundation,” despite both possessing divine revelation. This mutual denial exposed their bias and loss of sincerity toward truth. 
How does Allah create when He decrees something? 
He works gradually over time - False 
He says 'Be,' and it is - True 
He needs help from angels - False 
Explanation: Verse 117 beautifully captures Allah’s creative power: when He wills something, “He only says Be, and it is.” This affirms that creation occurs by His command alone without process, partner, or delay.
