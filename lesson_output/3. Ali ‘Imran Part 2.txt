Have you ever swum against a strong current? 
The harder you fight, the more tired you get. But when you move with the flow, you find peace and strength. 
Faith is the same, real surrender is not weakness, but trusting Allah completely, letting His will guide your heart with calm.
After the victory at Badr, some tribes like Banū Qaynuqā‘ became proud and mocked the Prophet ﷺ. Allah reminded them that pride always leads to loss. <i>Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr</i> explains that this warning showed that no one can escape Allah’s power. The battle of Badr was a sign that victory comes only from Allah, not from numbers.
These verses also remind us that wealth, beauty, and status are only short-lived. <i>Al-Qurṭubī</i> teaches that real value lies in Allah’s pleasure (<i>Riḍwān</i>). Allah praises those who seek forgiveness before dawn, a sign of true faith and humility. He ends by declaring that the only true religion with Him is Islam - submission to His will, as taught by all prophets.
