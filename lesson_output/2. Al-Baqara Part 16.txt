Allah gives boundaries that nurture justice and protect the believer. These limits are not restrictions but signs of His care. They teach us that true freedom grows through obedience. 
The verses show how divine law shapes hearts and guides us into a life of clarity and mercy.
In these verses Allah teaches rules that protect family rights, life and worship. The guidance begins with wills and inheritance so families act with honesty and avoid arguments. According to Ibn Kathir the verse warns against altering a bequest after hearing it. When we move through the passage the focus shifts to fasting and the month of Ramadan. 
Al Razi notes that these rulings remind believers that hardship is lifted when illness or travel occurs. The verses then address the Sacred Months and the conduct of fighting. Classical tafasir explain that fighting is only allowed when believers are attacked and never for wrongdoing. 
These connected commands build a clear picture that mercy and justice flow together in Allah’s law.

As protective guidelines that create space for growth - True 
As strict punishments for wrongdoing - False 
As rules that exist only to limit actions - False 
Explanation: The lesson explains that divine boundaries are protective guidelines that create space for spiritual growth and community harmony. 
What makes boundaries 'sacred' according to these verses? 
They are preserved by respected communities - False 
They come from Allah who is All Wise - True 
They are supported by learned scholars - False 
Explanation: The lesson emphasizes that boundaries are sacred because they come from Allah, the All-Wise, who knows what protects and what harms.

Explanation: The lesson teaches that the servant calls upon Allah directly without any intermediary. Allah hears and responds as He wills.
