Imagine holding onto a rope that keeps you safe from falling. That rope is Allah’s guidance found in the Qur’an.
These verses remind us that being the best Ummah is not about name or number, but about living truthfully, staying united, and spreading goodness through our actions and hearts.
Allah ﷻ calls believers to hold firmly to His rope, meaning the Qur’an and the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ, and not divide among themselves. Unity gives strength, while division weakens hearts and faith.
According to <i>Ibn Kathīr</i>, this honor of being “the best nation” (3:110) belongs to those who sincerely act for good, forbid wrong, and uphold justice. Similarly, <i>Al-Rāzī</i> explains that true power lies in collective faith, compassion, and moral strength, not in wealth or position. <i>Maʿārif al-Qurʾān</i> adds that holding the rope means staying close to divine revelation and prophetic teachings, which unite hearts, nurture mercy, and spread light to others.
When believers live by these values, their unity reflects the beauty of Islam to the world.
