When we make mistakes, Allah never abandons us.
In these verses, we see the first story of hope after the first sin, when divine mercy meets human regret. It reminds us that no matter how far we fall, sincere repentance can always lead us back to Allah’s love and forgiveness.
After Adam’s mistake, Allah did not reject him. Instead, He taught Adam words of repentance: “Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves...” - the first act of <i>tawbah</i> in human history (Ibn Kathir, Al-Razi). This moment teaches that guidance often begins after failure.
Allah promised that those who follow His guidance “will have no fear and no grief” (Ma‘arif al-Qur’an), meaning inner peace belongs to those who turn back to Him.
The verses also turn to the Children of Israel, reminding them of their blessings and warning against preaching what they do not practice (Al-Razi). 
True faith requires consistency, living by the guidance we share with others and honoring the covenant with Allah through action and sincerity.
What did Allah do first when Adam made his mistake? 
Taught him words of repentance - True 
Punished him immediately - False 
Asked the angels to help him - False 
Explanation: “Allah taught Adam the words of repentance, showing that divine mercy precedes punishment, the first lesson of <i>tawbah</i> in human history.” 
What makes prayer feel light and joyful instead of heavy? 
Having khushu - a humble, peaceful heart - True 
Performing prayer together with many other people - False 
Using beautiful prayer mats and surroundings - False 
Explanation: “Verses 45–46 teach that prayer feels light only for those who have <i>khushu, </i>deep humility and awareness of standing before Allah, certain of meeting Him one day.” 
What was special about Adam's repentance in human history? 
It was the first act of tawbah ever - True 
It was done in a special place - False 
It required many witnesses - False 
Explanation: “According to <i>Ibn Kathir</i> and <i>Al-Razi</i>, Adam’s repentance was humanity’s first act of <i>tawbah</i> — a sincere return to Allah through regret, confession, and seeking mercy.”
