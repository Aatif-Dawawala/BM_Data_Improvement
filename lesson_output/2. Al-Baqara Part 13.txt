Life’s greatest teacher is not comfort, but trial.
In these verses, Allah unveils that every hardship refines faith, every loss deepens trust, and every struggle brings believers closer to Him, if they respond with patience and remembrance.
Allah announces trials, fear, loss, hunger, pain—before they arrive, as Al-Rāzī notes, to prepare hearts for endurance, not despair.
The repeated shift of the <i>Qiblah</i> carries layers of wisdom (Ibn Kathīr), completing divine guidance and distinguishing sincerity from pride.
Ibn Juzayy records that some People of the Scripture recognized the Prophet ﷺ as clearly as their own sons (ʿAbdullāh ibn Salām), yet pride veiled obedience. Amid these commands, Allah calls believers to remembrance with the heart, tongue, and deed (Maʿārif al-Qur’ān), promising: “Remember Me, and I will remember you.” 
When calamity strikes, <i>“Innā lillāhi wa innā ilayhi rājiʿūn”</i> is not sorrow, it is surrender and serenity.
What does Allah promise to those who say 'We belong to Allah and to Him we return' during hardship? 
His blessings, mercy, and guidance - True 
Immediate relief from all problems - False 
Protection from future tests - False 
Explanation: Verse 156–157 explain that those who respond to calamity with “Innā lillāhi wa innā ilayhi rāji‘ūn” receive three divine gifts: Allah’s salawāt (special blessings and forgiveness), His rahmah (mercy), and His hudā (guidance). 
Why does Allah announce trials before they happen? 
To prepare hearts for endurance rather than despair - True 
To frighten people into obedience - False 
To show His power over creation - False 
Explanation: As explained in the tafsīr of Fakhr al-Rāzī and Ma‘ārif al-Qur’ān, Allah reveals the reality of trials beforehand to prepare the believer’s soul for ṣabr (patience), turning hardship into readiness not fear. 
What direction should Muslims face when praying? 
Toward the Sacred Mosque - True 
Toward Jerusalem - False 
Any direction they choose - False 
Explanation: Verses 149–150 command believers to face the Ka‘bah in Makkah, symbolizing unity of faith and obedience to divine command, regardless of where they are. 
Did the People of the Scripture hide some truth they knew? 
Yes - True 
No - False 
Explanation: Verse 146 affirms that some of the People of the Scripture (Ahl al-Kitāb) recognized the Prophet ﷺ as clearly as their own children but concealed the truth out of envy and pride, as noted by Ibn Kathīr and Ibn Juzayy.
