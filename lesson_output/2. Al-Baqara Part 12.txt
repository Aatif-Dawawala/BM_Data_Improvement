Faith is not inherited, it is lived.
These verses of Surah Al-Baqarah teach that devotion to Allah is a personal covenant. Each soul must stand before its Lord on its own merit, not on ancestry or inherited identity.
The Qur’an paints two powerful scenes of accountability.
First, Prophet Yaʿqūb (ʿalayhi as-salām) asks his sons, <i>“What will you worship after me?”</i> - his dying reminder that faith outweighs worldly inheritance (Ibn Kathīr). Their answer affirmed pure <i>tawḥīd</i>, proving that salvation cannot be borrowed (Al-Rāzī).
Then, the command to change the <i>Qiblah</i> tested early Muslims, separating the sincere from the doubtful (Maʿārif al-Qur’ān). That moment defined them as <i>ummatan wasaṭan</i>- a balanced nation of justice and harmony (Ibn Juzayy). Allah reassured them: <i>“Never would Allah cause your faith to be lost.”</i>
No sincere obedience is ever wasted.
What was Prophet Ya‘qub’s final question to his sons before his death? 
What will you worship after I am gone? - True 
Who will lead the family business? - False 
How will you divide my wealth? - False 
Explanation: Prophet Ya‘qub’s final concern was his sons’ faith, not wealth or leadership. He asked, “What will you worship after me?” showing that true legacy is spiritual guidance, not material inheritance. 
Can a person inherit faith from their ancestors? 
Yes - False 
No - True 
Explanation: The Qur’an repeats, “What they earned belongs to them, and what you earn belongs to you.” This teaches that faith and righteousness cannot be inherited each believer is responsible for their own deeds. 
Whom did Ya‘qub’s sons promise to worship? 
The one God of Abraham, Ishmael, and Isaac - True 
Multiple gods like their neighbors - False 
The gods of Egypt - False 
Explanation: Ya‘qub’s sons replied, “We will worship your God and the God of your fathers Abraham, Ishmael, and Isaac one God.” This affirms tawḥīd (oneness of Allah), the shared message of all prophets. 
What command from Allah tested the faith of the early Muslims? 
The change of prayer direction (qiblah) - True 
The command to perform Hajj - False 
The prohibition of certain foods - False 
Explanation: Allah changed the qiblah from Jerusalem to the Ka‘bah in Makkah to test obedience. It separated those who followed the Messenger ﷺ in full submission from those who turned away in doubt. 
What does “ummatan wasaṭan” (a justly balanced nation) describe? 
A community that avoids extremes and shows balance - True 
A community that is wealthy and powerful - False 
A community that speaks Arabic - False 
Explanation: Allah called Muslims an “ummatan wasaṭan” a community of balance, justice, and moderation. This means Muslims should live between extremes, showing fairness, mercy, and truth in all actions.
