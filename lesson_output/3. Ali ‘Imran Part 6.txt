Have you ever watched light break through thick clouds after a storm?
 Confusion is like that darkness but Allah’s truth always shines through. 
These verses remind us that every miracle, from Adam’s creation to ʿĪsā’s birth, points back to the same Source: the One who says <i>“Be!”</i> and it is.
When confusion spread among the people of Najrān about ʿĪsā (AS), Allah revealed these verses to restore clarity. They teach us how to think — not just what to believe. <i>Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr</i> explains that the comparison between ʿĪsā and Ādam silences false claims by reminding us that Allah’s power is beyond human logic. He creates without cause, by His word alone.
But this story also speaks to our hearts. Like the people of that time, we too face doubts and questions. The Qur’an shows that truth is not about debate, but about submission. Faith deepens when we stop asking “how” and start seeing “Who.” Every sign, birth, life, and miracle points back to the One Creator.

We will be God's helpers; we believe in God - True 
They demanded proof of his message first - False 
What does “Allah is the Best of Planners” teach us? 
Allah's wise plan always defeats evil schemes - True 
Allah and humans scheme in the same way - False 
Scheming is always wrong in Islam - False
