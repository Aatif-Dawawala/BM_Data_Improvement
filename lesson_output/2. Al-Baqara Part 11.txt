Every soul that claims faith will be tested.
Through Prophet Ibrahim’s life, Allah shows that nearness and leadership are not inherited, they are earned through obedience, patience, and absolute submission to His command. True honour before Allah comes from action, not ancestry.
Among the greatest moments in human history was when Prophet Ibrahim (alayhi as-salam) was tested with divine commands which he fulfilled completely. 
Ibn Kathir explains these trials as leaving his homeland, debating Nimrod, facing the fire, and offering his son. 
Al Razi adds that fulfilling these tests made him an imam by righteousness, not lineage. When Allah said, “My covenant does not include the wrongdoers,” He affirmed that authority in religion is moral, not tribal.
Ibrahim and Isma’il then built the Ka’bah, the spiritual center uniting all Muslims, praying humbly, “Our Lord, accept from us,” and asking for a messenger among their descendants. This was fulfilled through Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.
What made Allah promise to make Ibrahim a leader of people? 
He fulfilled all the tests Allah gave him - True 
He was born into a righteous family - False 
He was the oldest prophet of his time - False 
Explanation: Verse 124 shows that Allah made Ibrahim a leader after he successfully fulfilled all the divine tests (kalimāt) given to him, proving perfect obedience and faith. 
What did Allah mean when He said His pledge doesn't hold for wrongdoers? 
Leadership depends on your own actions, not family status - True 
Only Ibrahim's direct children could be leaders - False 
The Ka'bah is forbidden to evil people - False 
Explanation: Verse 124 teaches that Allah’s covenant of leadership is based on righteousness and justice, not family lineage. Even descendants of prophets cannot claim divine favor without obedience. 
What did Ibrahim ask Allah after being promised leadership? 
Will You make leaders from my descendants too? - True 
Will my people accept this leadership? - False 
How long will this leadership last? - False 
Explanation: In verse 124, Ibrahim humbly asked Allah whether this honor of leadership (<i>imāmah</i>) would also include his descendants. 
Why did Allah make the House a resort for people? 
So people would keep returning to it - True 
To make it the most beautiful building - False 
To hold the largest gatherings possible - False 
Explanation: Verse 125 describes the Ka‘bah as a ‘mathābah,’ a place of return where hearts are drawn repeatedly by divine love and longing. 
Did Ibrahim and Jacob give the same advice to their sons? 
Yes - True 
No - False 
Explanation: Verse 132 shows that both Ibrahim and Ya‘qub instructed their sons to remain steadfast in Islam and never die except in submission to Allah.
