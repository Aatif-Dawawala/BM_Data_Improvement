Faith is tested not only in hardship but in obedience.
 In these verses (2:61–72), the Children of Israel show how hesitation and distrust can turn a simple act into a heavy burden.
 Their story reminds us that real faith begins with trust, a heart ready to obey even before understanding why.
Allah commanded the Israelites to sacrifice a cow, but instead of obeying, they questioned endlessly, its age, color, and purpose (Ibn Kathir, Al-Razi). Each question revealed more hesitation than faith, until their stubbornness made a simple task nearly impossible.
Later, Allah used the sacrificed cow as part of a miracle. A murdered man was struck with part of it, and he came back to life, revealing the killer (Ma‘arif al-Qur’an). This proved Allah’s full power over life and death.
Before this, their disobedience breaking the Sabbath, mocking commands, and craving worldly food over heavenly gifts brought humiliation (Al-Razi, Ibn Juzay). 
Their story teaches that obedience without excuses is the key to true faith.
